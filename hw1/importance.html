Why It Matters

Data Visualization in Open Science & Space Science

Data visualization helps researchers communicate complex results quickly. This is especially important in open science and space science, where datasets can be large and difficult to interpret.

  • Open Science
    • Visualizations make shared datasets easier to understand, so other researchers can reuse data without needing to start from scratch.
    • When results are shown clearly, scientists can compare studies faster and avoid repeating expensive or unnecessary experiments.
    • Charts and dashboards help people spot trends and errors sooner, which improves transparency and makes research more trustworthy.
  • Space Science
    • Space research often includes many variables, technical terms, and measurements; visualizing that information reduces confusion and helps teams focus on what matters.
    • Instead of reading long reports and tables, scientists can use visuals to quickly interpret changes in control variables, sensor readings, or simulation outputs.
    • Clear visual communication makes it easier for different space-related fields (engineering, astronomy, planetary science, climate science) to collaborate and understand each other’s results.
  • Graphics
    • Even when writing is simplified, thousands of samples or measurements are overwhelming to read in raw form; graphics summarize the results in a way people can process quickly.
    • Visualizations highlight the “big picture” (patterns, clusters, outliers), which helps researchers understand outcomes and form better questions for the next experiment.
    • Graphs, maps, and plots allow scientists to share results with wider audiences, including students and the public, without losing the main meaning.
