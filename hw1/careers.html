Getting Started & Careers

Careers Connected to Data Visualization

Below are four common career paths that use data visualization. Each role includes a short description, a few steps to get started, and examples of companies that hire for the position.

Data Analyst

A data analyst turns raw data into charts and reports to help teams make decisions.

Steps to get there

  1. Learn Excel/Sheets and basic chart types.
  2. Practice with SQL and simple dashboards.
  3. Build a small portfolio (2-3 projects).

Companies that hire

  • Google
  • Amazon
  • Target

Data Scientist

A data scientist uses statistics and machine learning, then visualizes results to explain findings.

Steps to get there

  1. Learn Python and visualization libraries (Matplotlib/Seaborn).
  2. Study statistics and basic machine learning.
  3. Do projects using real datasets (Kaggle or open data).

Companies that hire

  • Microsoft
  • Netflix
  • Meta

Data Visualization Engineer

A visualization engineer builds interactive charts and dashboards, often for websites or internal tools.

Steps to get there

  1. Learn HTML/CSS/JavaScript basics.
  2. Practice with a chart library (D3.js or Plotly).
  3. Create interactive demo projects for your portfolio.

Companies that hire

  • Tableau (Salesforce)
  • Adobe
  • IBM

Research Scientist

A research scientist analyzes experimental data and uses visuals to communicate results clearly.

Steps to get there

  1. Choose a field (space, climate, biology, etc.) and learn its data types.
  2. Learn Python/R and make clear plots from research datasets.
  3. Join a lab or research project (school or internship).

Companies & organizations

  • NASA
  • JPL
  • Universities / research labs
